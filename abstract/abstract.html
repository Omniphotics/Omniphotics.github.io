About Omniphotics

A brief abstract and demo

What are Omniphotics?

Have you ever worn sunglasses while having to go back and forth between indoors and outdoors? If you have, you’ll know that it's hard to see inside because of how dark they are. Your only solution to this would be to take them off every time you walk inside and put them back on everytime you walk outside. It’s a hassle, and needs to be fixed. Omniphotics are the solution. Omniphotics provide you with dynamically adaptive lighting based on the user’s preference. The wearer is able to rotate the lenses to create a darker or lighter tint depending on what they want. This would prevent people from having to switch glasses or keep removing and wearing them. The name Omniphotics comes from the root omni meaning “all” and photic meaning “light”, because these are truly designed to cover a wide range of light levels. Omniphotics utilize a simple and sleek design to give wearers function and fashion. They’re meant to be comfortable and useful, and keep not only your head comfortable, but your eyes too. Omniphotics innovate scientifically, technologically, artistically, and mathematically to provide users with an amazing experience.

Sources & References

Omniphotics was a project that would not have been possible without lots of help from various online sources and explanations. Below I have included links and descriptions to all of the places I found my information. You can click the titles of the sources to be redirected straight to them.

"Bell's Theorem: The Quantum Venn Diagram Paradox" video by minutephysics and 3Blue1Brown

This video by YouTube channels minutephysics and 3Blue1Brown was the inspiration behind Omniphotics. In the beginning of the video they show the base tech behind Omniphotics, and explain a lot of the light physics. The video goes more in depth into the quantum mechanics and bizarre nature of photons. It's a fantastic watch and serves as the backbone for the science, technology, and inspiration behind Omniphotics.

"Some Light Quantum Mechanics" video by 3Blue1Brown and minutephysics

This is the second part to the video above. This video is more about the math and technicalities of photons and their behavior as waves. A lot of the information provided in the math section of the Omniphotics website was provided by this video, so if you'd like more detail on those topics I would high recommend giving it a watch.

Polarizaton Tutorial Article by the Physics Classroom

This is a great lower level explanation if the above two are too much for you. The Physics Classroom provides an excellent high school level explanation of polarizing filters and how the science behind them works. The article provides simple diagrams and long in-depth yet simple and understandable explanations.

Polarization Physics Article by Lumen Learning

Lumen Learning provides another in-depth article on the science and physics behind polarization and polarizing filters. This article is a nice middleground; it's slightly more advanced than the previous article by The Physics Classroom but less advanced than the first two videos from 3Blue1Brown and minutephysics.
