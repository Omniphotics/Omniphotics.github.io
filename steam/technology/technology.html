The Top of the Ladder

Technology is the highest in the hierarchy of the main four STEAM disciplines. At the base is math, the purest and most fundamental discipline. Science then builds on top of that and applies the math in physical and realistic ways. Engineering is the planning and fundamental design of how to use science to finally create the final product: technology. So what is the technology behind Omniphotics?

Simply put, the technology in Omniphotics is the application of the science and engineering. Omniphotics use a dual-polarization lense system to dynamically and adaptively block out light. The technology used is the two polarized filters in each lense and the plastic frame that holds them. Another part of the technology is that one of the polarized lenses is fixed in place on each side, and the other has a small bump to rotate it with.

A Polarization Simulation

Drag the triangle below in circles to simulate a dual-polarization filter!

Notice how the brightness is the hightest when the triangle is on the left or right, and lowest when the triangle is on the top or bottom. This simulates how dual-polarization works in real life; each multiple of 90° is one full cycle of brightness.
